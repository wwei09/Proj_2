Tribute to Mark Zuckerberg

 

Mark Zuckerberg is a well know billionaire because he’s the co-founder of Facebook, and Facebook is now one of the popular social media that people well know. But he is also a computer programmer, the richest entrepreneurs under age of 40, and philanthropists.

Mark Zuckerberg is known as co- founder of Facebook and also a computer programmer. Mark began to use the computer and write program since he was in middle school. When he was 12 he used Atari BASIC to create a messaging program he named "Zucknet." for his father, it allows the computer between house and dental office to communicate with pinging each other. Zuckerberg later attend Harvard University, at his sophomore year, he built a program called “CourseMatch” that let students pick their classes based on what their friend picked. At that time Mark was one of the most wildly known software programmers on the campus. Because of his programming skills, latter while he still in college he and his friends created a website that allowed users to create their profiles, upload their photos, and communicate with others on the site, and this is what we known today--- Facebook.

Mark Zuckerberg is also one of the youngest billionaires in American under age of 40. Even this is all because he found Facebook, but without his great programming skills, Facebook will not still exist today. Now Facebook is one of the world's well know social media site, changed from a few user website to know a social media site that has more than a billion users. From a company launched with few employees to known have more than 17 thousand workers. Facebook not just brought wealth to Mark Zuckerberg, but also created thousands of jobs in and out of America.

Other than the title of computer programmer and billionaire, Mark Zuckerberg is also a well-known philanthropist. Just until the end of 2016, Mark Zuckerberg have been giving out more than $1,600 million for charity. Over the past few years, Mark Zuckerberg and his wife Priscilla Chen have donated 99 percent of their Facebook fortune to the several charities to help education and environments.

Mark Zuckerberg a computer programmer, one of the youngest entrepreneurs under 40, and philanthropists. A man built Facebook and later in his life use what he earned donated to charities for goods of others.
